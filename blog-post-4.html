Not Every Surface Should Be Cleaned the Same Way

By NovaLuxe Team• 6 min read• June 2026

One of the biggest misconceptions about cleaning is that a single product can be used everywhere. This assumption — common even among well-meaning homeowners — is one of the leading causes of surface damage, residue buildup, and premature wear in luxury homes.

Different materials require different care. Wood, stone, stainless steel, glass, and specialty finishes all react differently to cleaning products and techniques. Using the wrong product can leave residue, cause discoloration, strip protective coatings, or even permanently damage surfaces over time — a costly mistake in a home where every finish was chosen with care.

Surface-Specific Best Practices

Professional cleaners are trained to identify the safest and most effective products for each area of the home. A pH-neutral cleaner perfect for marble countertops may be entirely inappropriate for stainless steel appliances or hardwood floors. The right product, applied correctly, makes all the difference between a surface that ages gracefully and one that deteriorates prematurely.

"The right product, used correctly and in the right quantity, is the difference between cleaning and caring for your home."

Best Practices for Home Maintenance

To help maintain a professionally cleaned home, consider these simple habits:

  • Clean spills promptly — the longer they sit, the deeper they penetrate.
  • Use soft microfiber cloths instead of abrasive scrubbers on delicate surfaces.
  • Never mix cleaning products — chemical reactions can be dangerous.
  • Follow manufacturer recommendations for delicate surfaces like natural stone and hardwood.
  • Less is more — excess product creates residue that attracts more dirt over time.

For more information about safer cleaning practices and products, visit the EPA Safer Choice Products Guide.
